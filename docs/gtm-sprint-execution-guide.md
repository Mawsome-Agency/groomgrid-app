**Target:** 100 paying subscribers by April 30, 2026

## Executive Summary

This playbook uses **direct outreach** as the primary acquisition channel — we cannot rely on SEO (current: 1 organic session/week). The strategy focuses on:

1. **Facebook Group Engagement** — Where groomers actually hang out (3 target groups, ~50K+ combined members)
2. **Groomer Influencer Partnerships** — Micro-influencers with 5-50K followers
3. **Referral Program** — Leverage early adopters to bring peers
4. **Beta User Feedback Loop** — First 10 users become case studies

**Math to 100:**
- Week 1 (Apr 7-13): 15 signups → 10 paid after trial
- Week 2 (Apr 14-20): 30 signups → 20 paid after trial
- Week 3 (Apr 21-27): 35 signups → 25 paid after trial
- Week 4 (Apr 28-30): 20 signups → 15 paid after trial
- **Total: 100 signups → 70+ paid by May 15** (assuming 70% trial-to-paid conversion)

## 1. Channel Prioritization

### Primary Channels (95% of effort)

#### A. Facebook Groomer Groups (40% of effort)
**Target Groups:**
1. **Dog Groomers Only Forum** — ~30K members, highly active
2. **The Daily Groomer** — ~15K members, professional focus
3. **The Everyday Pet Groomer** — ~8K members, independent groomers

**Why These Groups:**
- High concentration of target ICP (independent mobile groomers, salon owners)
- Members actively discuss business problems (scheduling, payments, no-shows)
- Organic, community-driven conversations (not sales-y)

**Engagement Strategy:**
- First 3 days: Lurk, identify top contributors, like/comment on 20+ posts
- Join conversations with value, not pitches
- Share ONE value-add post/week about business management tips
- DM engaged members who mention scheduling/payment pain points

#### B. Groomer Influencer Partnerships (35% of effort)
**Target Profile:**
- 5K-50K followers on Instagram/TikTok
- Active posting (3-5x/week)
- Authentic, not overly promotional
- Focus on grooming tips, business advice, or day-in-the-life

**Outreach Numbers:**
- Week 1: Identify 30 influencers
- Week 2: DM 20 with partnership offer
- Week 3-4: Secure 5-8 partners for shoutouts/collabs

**Partnership Structure:**
- Option A: Free 6-month GroomGrid access + shoutout post
- Option B: $100-200 flat fee for dedicated review/demo video
- Option C: Affiliate — 30% of first-year revenue from referred signups

#### C. Referral Program (20% of effort)
**Structure:**
- For referrers: $50 Stripe credit per successful signup (paid after trial converts)
- For new signups: First month 50% off ($14.50 instead of $29)
- Easy sharing: Unique referral link with tracking

**Technical Implementation:**
```
/ref/{referral_code} → redirects to signup with promo code
Promo code: FIRSTHALF (50% off first month)
Referral tracking in Stripe customer metadata
```

### Secondary Channels (5% of effort)

#### D. Instagram/TikTok Organic (3%)
- Repurpose influencer content
- Post grooming business tips (scheduling hacks, payment reminders)
- Behind-the-scenes of GroomGrid development

#### E. Email to Waitlist (2%)
- Any waitlist signups get personalized welcome
- Offer beta access + discount code
- Ask for 15-min feedback call

## 4. Referral Program Structure

### The Offer
**For Referrers:**
- $50 Stripe account credit per successful signup
- Credit applied after referred user completes 14-day trial and converts to paid
- Unlimited referrals — no cap on earnings

**For New Signups:**
- 50% off first month ($14.50 instead of $29)
- Applied automatically via promo code: FIRSTHALF
- Can be combined with beta pricing

### Technical Setup

**Stripe Configuration:**
```bash
# Create promo code
curl -X POST https://api.stripe.com/v1/promotion_codes \
  -u $STRIPE_SECRET_KEY: \
  -d coupon=first_half \
  -d code=FIRSTHALF \
  -d max_redemptions=1000
```

**Referral URL Structure:**
```
https://getgroomgrid.com/signup?ref={referral_code}&promo=FIRSTHALF
```

**Customer Metadata:**
```json
{
  "referral_code": "JANE123",
  "referred_by": "jane@example.com",
  "promo_used": "FIRSTHALF"
}
```

## 5. ICP Validation Integration

### Target Users for First 10 Beta Interviews

**Ideal Profile:**
- Independent mobile groomer OR salon owner with 2-5 staff
- Currently using Google Calendar, pen/paper, or basic scheduling software
- Experiencing pain with: double bookings, payment chasing, no-shows
- Active in Facebook groomer groups
- Willing to give 15-20 minutes for feedback

### Beta User Recruitment Script

#### Facebook DM Invite:
```
Hey [Name], 

I noticed you're an experienced groomer and you've been active in the groups — would you be interested in trying a new tool I'm building?

I'm looking for 10 groomers to test GroomGrid (scheduling, payments, client management) and give me honest feedback. 

Free access for 30 days, plus I'll do a 15-min call to hear what you think. 

Your input will literally shape what I build next.

Interested? Let me know and I'll send you the link!

Best, 
[Your Name]
```

### Feedback Call Script (15 minutes)

**Opening (2 min):**
"Thanks so much for trying GroomGrid! I really appreciate you taking the time. I have about 10-15 questions — should only take 15 minutes. Sound good?"

**Questions:**
1. "Before we dive in — what are you currently using for scheduling and client management?" (1 min)
2. "What's the biggest pain point with your current setup?" (1 min)
3. "When you first tried GroomGrid, what was your first impression?" (1 min)
4. "What feature did you find most useful?" (1 min)
5. "Was anything confusing or frustrating?" (1 min)
6. "How much time do you think GroomGrid saved you (if any)?" (1 min)
7. "What would make you switch from your current tool to GroomGrid?" (1 min)
8. "What's one thing you wish GroomGrid did that it doesn't?" (1 min)
9. "What would you expect to pay for this monthly?" (30 sec)
10. "Would you recommend this to other groomers? Why or why not?" (30 sec)
11. "Anything else you want to share?" (1 min)

**Closing (2 min):**
"Thank you so much — this is incredibly helpful. I'll take all this feedback and use it to improve the product. As a thank you, I'll give you 3 free months of GroomGrid. Is there anything else you need from me?"
