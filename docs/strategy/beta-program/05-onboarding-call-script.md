# GroomGrid Founding Groomer Onboarding Call Script

**Duration:** 30 minutes
**Goal:** Get the tester set up, capture initial impressions, identify their specific use case
**Who:** Sofia or Lucia (Strategy & Planning team)
**When:** Within 48 hours of tester signing up

---

## Pre-Call Prep (5 min)

Before the call:
1. Pull up their GroomGrid account — verify email, check if they've logged in
2. Check their signup source (Reddit, Facebook, DM)
3. Prepare screen share if needed (they may want to see features)
4. Have the setup guide handy: `docs/strategy/beta-program/02-setup-guide.md`

---

## 0-5 min: Build Rapport

**Opening:**
> "Hey [Name]! Thanks so much for joining the beta. Before we dive into the app, I'd love to hear about your grooming business. How long have you been grooming? What kind of setup do you have — mobile, salon, home-based?"

**Listen for and note:**
- Business type (mobile / salon / home-based / cat specialist)
- Years of experience
- Number of clients per week
- Current tools they use (paper, Google Calendar, MoeGo, etc.)
- Pain points they mention organically (don't prompt yet)

**Transition:**
> "That's really helpful context. I want to make sure we set this up in a way that actually fits your day."

---

## 5-15 min: Guided Setup

**Walk through setup together (screen share or over-the-phone):**

### Step 1: Add a real client
> "Let's add your first real client. Not a test one — someone you're actually seeing this week. We'll put in their name, phone number, and their pet's details."

**Guide them to:**
- Client → Add Client → Name, Phone, Email
- Add pet → Pet name, breed, notes
- Encourage breed-specific notes (e.g., "nervous with nail trims," "requires muzzle")
- Note: They should add a REAL client, not a test one

### Step 2: Book a real appointment
> "Now let's book an actual appointment. When's your next client coming in?"

**Guide them to:**
- Appointments → New → Select client + pet → Set date/time
- Show the SMS reminder option (enabled by default)
- Point out the confirmation they'd receive

### Step 3: Show payments (optional)
> "If you want, we can set up payments too. But this is optional during beta — you can skip it and add it later."

### Step 4: Quick tour
> "Let me show you three things really quickly:"
1. **Dashboard** — today's appointments, upcoming week, revenue (if payments enabled)
2. **Pet profiles** — breed notes, vaccination status, behavior history
3. **Settings** — where to find notification preferences, payment setup

---

## 15-25 min: Let Them Explore

> "Okay, now it's your turn. Take 5-10 minutes to click around. Add another client, book another appointment, explore whatever catches your eye. I'll stay on — just narrate what you're thinking as you go."

**Listen for and note:**
- ❓ "Where is...?" → Navigation confusion
- 😕 "I wish this did..." → Feature gap
- 😊 "Oh, that's nice!" → Aha moment
- 😤 "This is annoying..." → UX friction
- 🤔 "How do I..." → Onboarding gap

**Don't interrupt or correct immediately.** Let them struggle a little — this is how we find UX issues.

---

## 25-30 min: Wrap Up

**Quick assessment:**
1. > "What's your first impression? Rate it 1-10."
2. > "What feature matters most to you right now?"
3. > "Anything confusing or missing that I should know about?"

**Explain what's next:**
- > "You'll get a weekly survey from me — just 3 questions, takes 5 minutes. Your feedback goes straight to the top of our list."
- > "If you hit any bugs or have ideas, just reply to my email. I'm a real person, not a bot. 😊"
- > "At the end of the 30 days, I'll ask for a 30-minute call just like this one to hear your full honest take."

**Close:**
> "Thanks again, [Name]. This is super valuable for us. Have a great rest of your day, and happy grooming!"
