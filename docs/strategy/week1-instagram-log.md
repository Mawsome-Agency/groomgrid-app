## 0. Instagram's Role in Our GTM Strategy

### Why Instagram Matters for GroomGrid

Instagram is our ICP's **#2 channel** (after Facebook Groups). Here's why it's critical:

| Factor | Evidence | Implication |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| **Visual medium** | Grooming is inherently visual — before/after transformations are the #1 content type | Natural content flywheel for groomers AND for software showcase |
| **Groomer discovery** | Pet owners find groomers via Instagram; groomers find tools via other groomers' Stories/Reels | Peer recommendation happens here |
| **Community norms** | Groomers post daily — it's part of their marketing routine | We enter an active, engaged community |
| **Mobile-first** | Our ICP (mobile groomers) lives on their phones between appointments | High overlap with our target user |
| **Hashtag ecosystems** | #doggrooming (8M+ posts), #mobilegrooming (500K+ posts) = massive discovery pools | Content can reach groomers beyond our immediate network |

### Channel Strategy Positioning

Per the REVISED-ACQUISITION-PLAN.md, Instagram falls between Community Engagement (Ch 1) and SEO/Content (Ch 3):

- **Phase 1 (Weeks 1-3):** Lurk, learn, engage as a genuine community member. Leave thoughtful comments. Build name recognition.
- **Phase 2 (Weeks 4-6):** Post GroomGrid-branded content (grooming business tips, scheduling hacks, software UI previews).
- **Phase 3 (Weeks 7+):** Groomer spotlight series, before/after templates, Reels showing the product in action.

**This document covers Phase 1 only.** Do NOT post any GroomGrid-branded content until Week 4.

## 3. ENGAGEMENT PLAN: 10 GENUINE COMMENTS

### Engagement Principles

Before drafting comments, here are the rules:

1. **NEVER mention GroomGrid by name** in Phase 1 comments. Zero product mentions.
2. **Be a real person.** Comments must reference specific details from the post — not generic "great job!" spam.
3. **Ask questions.** Questions get replies. Replies start relationships.
4. **Share experience.** Position as someone who understands grooming business challenges (brand strategist who works with pet businesses).
5. **Use the poster's name or handle.** Personalization matters.
6. **Keep it brief.** 1-3 sentences max. Instagram comments aren't essays.

### 10 Draft Comments (Ready to Post)

These are templates — **adapt each one to the specific post content before posting.** The key word is "genuine."

---

**Comment 1 — For a before/after transformation post**

> "The difference here is incredible! What clipper guard did you use on the body? That length looks perfect for this breed."

*Why it works:* Specific, asks a technique question, shows genuine interest in the craft. Groomers love talking technique.

---

**Comment 2 — For a mobile groomer's van/setup post**

> "Your van setup is so clean! How long did it take to dial in your organization system? I've heard from so many mobile groomers that the setup is half the battle."

*Why it works:* Acknowledges the specific challenge of mobile grooming. "I've heard from so many mobile groomers" subtly signals industry knowledge without being salesy.

---

**Comment 3 — For a post about scheduling or busy days**

> "Packed schedule! Do you build in buffer time between appointments for drive time and cleanup, or do you stack them tight? Always curious how mobile groomers handle the logistics."

*Why it works:* Directly addresses our core pain point (scheduling/logistics) as a genuine question. Plants the seed that scheduling is a discussable topic.

---

**Comment 4 — For a cat grooming post**

> "Love seeing cat grooming content — it's so underserved! How do you handle the temperament assessment on new cat clients? I imagine that first visit is everything."

*Why it works:* Validates the cat groomer's niche expertise. Asks about a real professional challenge (temperament tracking is in our pet profiles feature).

---

**Comment 5 — For a frustrated vent post about no-shows or late clients**

> "Ugh, no-shows are the worst — especially when you've blocked off drive time for a mobile visit. Have you tried any kind of reminder system or deposit policy? Curious what's worked for other mobile groomers."

*Why it works:* Directly addresses our #1 pain point (no-shows). Mentioning "reminder system" is a soft signal — it's a real solution groomers discuss, not a product pitch.

---

**Comment 6 — For a new groomer's progress post**

> "The progress is real! How long have you been grooming? That finish is clean for someone early in their career. Keep it up! 🔥"

*Why it works:* Encouraging, specific, targets the "Newbie Mobile Starter" sub-persona. Building rapport with new groomers = early adopters for affordable software.

---

**Comment 7 — For a salon owner's team post**

> "Love seeing a well-run salon team! How many groomers do you have on staff? Managing that many schedules must be its own full-time job."

*Why it works:* Targets the Salon Owner/Manager persona. Acknowledges scheduling complexity (our core value prop) as a relatable challenge.

---

**Comment 8 — For a creative grooming or breed-specific post**

> "This style is gorgeous! Do you find clients are willing to pay premium for specialized cuts like this, or is it mostly standard groom requests? The breed-specific work is so impressive."

*Why it works:* Validates premium pricing (which makes $29/mo software feel more affordable). Engages the "Breed Specialist" sub-persona.

---

**Comment 9 — For a tool/product review or haul post**

> "Nice haul! Always curious what tools groomers are loving lately. Have you tried any software for client management, or are you still rocking the paper-and-memory system? 😄"

*Why it works:* Directly relevant to our product but framed as a casual conversation. The "paper-and-memory" line is humorous and relatable — it's what most solo groomers actually do.

---

**Comment 10 — For a grooming business milestone post (new van, anniversary, etc.)**

> "Congrats on the milestone! 🎉 That takes real hustle. What's been the biggest surprise about running your own grooming business vs. just doing the grooming part?"

*Why it works:* Celebrates their achievement. The question — business management vs. grooming — is exactly the pain point GroomGrid solves. Their answer is valuable ICP intel.

## 4. BEFORE/AFTER POST INSPIRATION (FOR GROOMGRID PHASE 2 CONTENT)

### 5 Inspiration Posts — What Works on Grooming Instagram

These are archetypes to study and adapt for GroomGrid's own content in Phase 2+:

---

### Inspiration 1: "The Neglected Rescue Glow-Up"

**Format:** Side-by-side or swipe carousel. Matted, dirty dog → perfectly groomed, happy dog.

**Why it works:**
- Emotional narrative (rescue + transformation)
- Massive engagement (people share these like crazy)
- Demonstrates groomer skill and compassion
- Comments flood with "You're amazing!" and "Where are you located?"

**GroomGrid adaptation (Phase 2):** Show the *scheduling workflow* alongside the transformation. "This groomer went from double-booked chaos to fully organized in 2 weeks. Here's their before/after — both the dog AND the calendar." 

---

### Inspiration 2: "The Timelapse Full Groom"

**Format:** 15-60 second Reel, sped-up full groom with trending audio.

**Why it works:**
- Satisfying process content (people watch the whole thing)
- Reels get 2-3x more reach than static posts
- Shows technique, tool use, and the groomer's workflow
- High save rate (groomers save for reference)

**GroomGrid adaptation (Phase 2):** Create a Reel showing "A day in the life of a mobile groomer using GroomGrid" — split screen: one side showing the groom, other side showing the app managing the schedule, client text reminders firing, payment processing.

---

### Inspiration 3: "The Tool Setup / Van Tour"

**Format:** Carousel or short video showing a mobile groomer's van interior, organization system, and tool layout.

**Why it works:**
- Groomers LOVE seeing other groomers' setups (it's their version of desk tours)
- High save rate (practical reference)
- Comments are full of questions ("Where did you get that drying box?")
- Establishes authority and professionalism

**GroomGrid adaptation (Phase 2):** "Mobile groomer tech stack" — show GroomGrid on the phone/tablet mounted in the van, running the day's schedule, with a caption about going paperless.

---

### Inspiration 4: "The Client Reaction"

**Format:** Video of dog owner seeing their freshly groomed pet for the first time. Joy, surprise, happy tears.

**Why it works:**
- Pure emotional content
- Shareable beyond grooming community (pet owners share with other pet owners)
- Validates the groomer's value (the "why I do this" moment)
- Builds the groomer's personal brand

**GroomGrid adaptation (Phase 2):** "The text that makes it happen" — Show a mock SMS reminder going out, client confirming, groomer arriving on time, happy pickup. "Behind every happy pet owner is a groomer who showed up on time. GroomGrid handles the schedule so you can handle the groom."

---

### Inspiration 5: "The Business Milestone / Real Talk"

**Format:** Text overlay on selfie or van photo. "X months in business" or "Real talk about pricing" or "What I wish I knew starting out."

**Why it works:**
- Authentic, vulnerable, relatable
- Builds personal connection (followers feel like they know you)
- High comment engagement (people share their own stories)
- Positions the groomer as a thought leader, not just a technician

**GroomGrid adaptation (Phase 2):** "What I wish I knew about running a grooming business" carousel — each slide is a business lesson (schedule smarter, automate reminders, track client history) with GroomGrid as the solution woven in naturally.

## 10. RISKS & MITIGATIONS

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Coming across as spammy or salesy in comments | MEDIUM | HIGH — damages trust before it's built | Strict "no product mentions in Phase 1" rule. Every comment must be genuinely about the post content. |
| Account banned for aggressive following/commenting | LOW | HIGH | Stay under 30 comments/day and 50 follows/day. Instagram limits are ~200/day but conservative is safer. |
