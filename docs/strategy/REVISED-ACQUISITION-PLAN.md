## 2. Three Highest-ROI Acquisition Channels

Ranked by expected return per hour of effort:

### Channel 1: 🥇 Direct Community Engagement (Facebook Groups + Reddit)

**Why #1:** The original GTM playbook was RIGHT about the channel — it was wrong about the timeline. Community trust takes weeks to build, not days.

**Evidence:**
- Facebook Groups are where 70% of groomers discuss business tools
- Reddit /r/doggrooming has 50K+ members, active buying advice threads
- Peer recommendation is the #1 driver for niche SaaS adoption

**What we did wrong:** Posted 4 times in 3 groups with zero prior relationship building. That's not community engagement — that's spray-and-pray.

**ROI Estimate:** 3-5 signups/week after 3-week trust-building period, scaling to 10-15/week by Week 6.

**Effort:** 1.5-2 hours/day

### Channel 2: 🥈 Product-Led Growth (Free Tier → Paid Upsell)

**Why #2:** The "free trial only" model creates a cliff. Most groomers won't commit $29/mo without proving value first. A genuine free tier (limited but useful) drives signups and creates habitual use.

**Evidence:**
- "Free pet grooming software" has 270/mo search volume (SpyFu)
- MoeGo and DaySmart both use freemium/limited-free models
- SaaS conversion from free → paid averages 2-5%, but free users provide testimonials, referrals, and social proof

**ROI Estimate:** 5-10 signups/week once free tier launches, with 3-5% converting over 60 days.

**Effort:** One-time engineering effort (~1 sprint) + ongoing

### Channel 3: 🥉 SEO + Content Marketing

**Why #3:** It's slow (60-90 days for traction) but it's our only scalable, compounding channel. We've already started — 3 blog posts published. Need to keep publishing and wait for the domain to age.

**Evidence:**
- DaySmart gets 12,500 organic clicks/mo (SpyFu) — this channel works in this niche
- Pawfinity gets 3,100 organic clicks/mo with domain strength of 44
- "Pet grooming software" ecosystem: 510-1,000+ searches/mo across related terms
- Our domain is 1 month old — we need 2-3 more months minimum

**ROI Estimate:** 0-5 signups/week in May, scaling to 10-20/week by late June if content cadence holds.

**Effort:** 2-3 posts/week (Content team owns this)

## 4. Pricing Experiment Plan

### Current Pricing Problem

| Issue | Evidence | Risk |
|-------|----------|------|
| $29/mo Solo tier may be too high for solo mobile groomers | Most solo groomers gross $40-60K/year; $29/mo = $348/year = ~0.7% of revenue | Price sensitivity kills conversion |
| 14-day free trial too short | Grooming is weekly/biweekly cycle; 14 days may not show value | Users churn before experiencing full value |
| No free tier | "Free pet grooming software" = 270 searches/mo — demand exists | We're invisible to price-sensitive segment |
| Enterprise tier premature | We have 0 users. No one is buying $149/mo yet. | Wastes positioning energy |

### Recommended Pricing Structure (Experiment)

**Phase 1: Launch Pricing (May 15 – June 15)**

| Tier | Price | What's Included | Target User |
|------|-------|----------------|-------------|
| **Free** | $0/mo | 10 clients, basic scheduling, manual reminders, no AI | Newbie Mobile Starter, price-sensitive groomers |
| **Solo** | $19/mo (founding) → $29/mo after 50 signups | Unlimited clients, AI scheduling, auto-reminders, payments, pet profiles | Core ICP: Independent Mobile Groomer |
| **Salon** | $59/mo (founding) → $79/mo after 25 signups | Everything in Solo + multi-staff, analytics, team scheduling, reporting | Salon Owner/Manager |

**Phase 2: Optimize Pricing (June 16 – June 30)**

After 30+ data points on free → paid conversion:

| Decision Point | Metric | Action |
|----------------|--------|--------|
| If free-to-paid > 5% | 5%+ converting | Keep pricing. Scale acquisition. |
| If free-to-paid 2-5% | Below target | Test $15/mo Solo, extend trial to 30 days, add onboarding call |
| If free-to-paid < 2% | Pricing or value problem | Interview churned users. Consider $9/mo Starter tier. Re-examine product-market fit. |
| If no one hits the 10-client free limit | Free tier too generous | Reduce to 5 clients or 30-day time limit instead |

### Pricing Experiment Calendar

| Week | Experiment | Success Metric |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| W3 (May 15) | Launch founding pricing: Solo $19/mo | 10+ signups in first week |
| W4 | Monitor trial-to-paid at 14-day mark | >20% conversion |
| W5 | A/B test trial length: 14 days vs 30 days (via promo code) | 30-day shows >25% conversion |
| W6 | Analyze free tier usage patterns — are users hitting limits? | >50% of free users add 5+ clients |
| W7 | If conversion < 3%, test $9/mo Starter tier | Signups increase >50% |
| W9 | Lock in final pricing for Q3 | Data-backed decision |

### Freemium vs Free Trial: Recommendation

**Go with freemium (limited free tier), NOT just a free trial.**

Reasons:
1. "Free pet grooming software" has 270/mo search volume — these people want free, not trial
2. A genuine free tier lets groomers adopt GroomGrid as their daily tool without commitment
3. Free users provide testimonials, bug reports, and referral potential
4. Usage-based upgrade triggers (client limits, AI features) convert better than time-based expiry
5. Reduces risk perception — "$0 to try" vs "$29/mo but you can cancel"

**Engineering scope:** Add client count limit, gate AI features behind paid tier, add upgrade prompts. Estimated: 3-5 dev days.
