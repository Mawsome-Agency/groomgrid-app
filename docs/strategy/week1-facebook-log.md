## 3. COMMENT TEMPLATES — By Post Type

These are NOT copy-paste scripts. They are frameworks. **Always personalize the first line to the specific post you're commenting on.** The goal is to be the most helpful person in the thread.

### Template A: Responding to "Help with scheduling" posts

**Trigger:** Someone posts about being overwhelmed by their calendar, double-booking, or not knowing how to organize their day.

```
Ugh, I feel this SO much. I used to live in that chaos too.

The thing that changed it for me: I started blocking my calendar by appointment TYPE instead of just slotting names into time blocks. Full groom = 2 hours. Bath and brush = 45 min. Nail trim = 15 min walk-in.

Sounds obvious but I was treating every slot like a blank 1-hour window and constantly overrunning or sitting around waiting. Once I color-coded by service type, I could see my whole day at a glance and stopped double-booking myself.

Also — 15 minute buffers between appointments. Game changer for staying on schedule AND giving yourself a bathroom break. 😅

What's your current system? Paper? Google Calendar?
```

**Why this works:** Validates their pain, shares a specific actionable tip, ends with a question that invites conversation.

---

### Template B: Responding to "No-show frustration" posts

**Trigger:** Someone vents about clients not showing up, wasting their time/gas.

```
No-shows are the WORST, especially when you've driven 30 minutes to get there. 😤

Three things that helped me cut my no-show rate from like 15% down to under 5%:

1. **Confirmation texts 24 hours before.** Not a fancy system — just a text that says "Hi [Name], looking forward to seeing [Pet] tomorrow at [Time]! Reply to confirm or reschedule." You'd be amazed how many people genuinely forgot.

2. **Cancellation policy that I actually enforce.** I put it in my intake form and remind people at booking. First no-show = warning. Second = prepayment required. Third = dropped. Sounds harsh but it protects the clients who DO show up.

3. **Waitlist for cancelled slots.** I keep a short list of clients who want earlier appointments. When someone cancels, I text the waitlist and usually fill it within an hour.

The biggest mindset shift: no-shows aren't "just part of the job." They're a system problem you can fix.

How many are you averaging per week? It might be more fixable than you think.
```

**Why this works:** Three concrete tips in order of ease, validates the emotion, ends with a question that positions no-shows as solvable (not inevitable).

---

### Template C: Responding to "Payment chasing" posts

**Trigger:** Someone complains about clients who say "I'll Venmo you!" and then ghost, or about awkward follow-up conversations.

```
The "I'll Venmo you" ghost is REAL. 😩

I went through a phase where I was chasing $200-300 in unpaid invoices every month. Not lost clients — just late payers. It's the worst feeling because you already did the work!

What finally worked:

**Collect at booking, not after.** I switched to taking a deposit or full payment when they schedule. "Your spot is confirmed when payment comes through" — suddenly people remember to pay.

**For existing clients who resist prepay:** Send a payment link via text the MOMENT you finish the groom. Like, before you even put the dog back in the owner's hands. "All set! Here's your invoice — you can pay right from this link." Zero friction = zero delays.

**The awkward follow-up:** I used to hate this. Then I realized: I'm not asking for a favor. I'm collecting payment for services rendered. Reframing it in my head made the texts way less painful. "Hey! Just a reminder about the balance for [Pet]'s groom last [day]. You can pay here: [link]"

Honestly, automating the whole payment flow was the single biggest quality-of-life upgrade I've made in this business.

What's your current payment setup? Are you doing invoices, cash, Venmo?
```

**Why this works:** Empathy-first, then three escalating solutions (from easiest to most systematic), ends with a question that invites them to share their current process.

### Template D: Responding to "Software recommendations" or "What do you use?" posts

**⚠️ CRITICAL: Week 1 — DO NOT MENTION GROOMGRID. Respond as a fellow groomer sharing experience, not as a company rep.**

**Trigger:** Someone asks "What scheduling software do you use?" or "Is MoeGo worth it?"

```
I've tried a few and honestly it depends on what you need.

If you're a solo mobile groomer: the big platforms (MoeGo, DaySmart) are kind of overkill. You end up paying for features you'll never use, and the setup process is like a 2-hour onboarding call. If you've got time for that, great. I didn't.

What I'd look for if I were starting over:
- Mobile-first (you're in a van, not an office)
- Quick setup (under 10 minutes)
- Automated reminders (this is the feature that actually pays for itself)
- Payment processing built in
- Under $30/month for a solo operation

The biggest mistake I see people make is choosing the "most popular" option instead of the one that fits their actual workflow. What's your setup — solo, salon, mobile? That'll narrow it down a lot.
```

**Why this works:** Honest assessment of the landscape, helpful criteria, NO product name dropped, positions you as knowledgeable but not salesy. In Week 3, when we DO mention GroomGrid, this person will already trust you.

### Template E: Responding to "New groomer starting out" posts

**Trigger:** Someone posts about starting their own mobile grooming business, looking for advice.

```
Welcome to the wild world of mobile grooming! 🐕💨

A few things I wish someone told me in year one:

**Your grooming skill is maybe 40% of your success.** The rest is scheduling, client communication, payment collection, and record-keeping. Nobody tells you that in grooming school.

**"I'll just remember" is not a client management system.** You WILL forget which dog has allergies, which needs a muzzle, which owner wants mid-groom updates. Get a system going from Day 1, even if it's just a solid notes app.

**No-shows aren't "just part of the job."** Automated confirmation texts cut my no-show rate in half. Set this up before you take your first booking.

**Build buffer time into every appointment.** 15 minutes between clients. You'll overrun, you'll need a bathroom break, and traffic exists. Booking back-to-back is how you end up skipping lunch and doing a bad job by client #6.

**Collect payment upfront if you can.** Chasing money after you've already done the work is the worst feeling. A deposit system or payment-at-booking saves so much stress.

Feel free to DM me if you want to bounce ideas. I remember how overwhelming those first few months were!
```

**Why this works:** Genuine mentorship energy. Five specific tips. Offer to DM (builds relationship for Week 2+ outreach). No product mentions.

**Timing:** 9:00am MT (peak engagement window)  
**Type:** Value-add / discussion starter

```
Hey everyone! 👋

Been grooming mobile for a few years now and I finally figured out a scheduling system that doesn't make me want to throw my phone into traffic. Figured I'd share in case it helps anyone who's still in the chaos phase.

**My 3 non-obvious scheduling rules:**

1️⃣ **Buffer blocks between EVERY appointment.** 15 minutes minimum. I used to pack my schedule tight because "more dogs = more money." Turns out, overruns, no recovery time, and burnout by 2pm actually loses you money. The buffer catches overruns AND gives you a minute to breathe.

2️⃣ **Color-code by service type.** Full groom, bath and brush, nail trim, cat groom — each gets a color. I can look at my calendar and instantly see if my day is balanced or if I've accidentally booked five 2-hour grooms in a row (bad idea, don't recommend 😅).

3️⃣ **Confirmation texts 24 hours before.** Not optional. Not "I'll text if I remember." EVERY appointment gets a confirmation text. Cut my no-show rate from 15% to under 5%. That's literally hundreds of dollars a month back in my pocket.

The biggest shift for me was realizing that scheduling IS part of the job. You didn't start grooming to manage a calendar, but if the calendar's a mess, the grooming suffers too.

What's YOUR best scheduling hack? I'm always looking to steal good ideas 😂

#doggrooming #groomingbusiness #mobilegroomer #schedulingtips
```

**Why this works:**
- Genuine value (3 specific tips anyone can use immediately)
- Relatable language ("throw my phone into traffic")
- Ends with a question that drives comments
- Zero product mentions — pure credibility building
- Tags help with group discovery algorithms

## APPENDIX: Quick-Reference Decision Tree

```
You see a post. Should you comment?
│
├─ Is it about scheduling, no-shows, payments, or business management?
│  ├─ YES → Use the appropriate template (A-E). Personalize it. Comment.
│  └─ NO → Is it a relatable vent/rant about the business?
│     ├─ YES → Empathy-only comment. "Ugh, I feel this." No tips, no agenda.
│     └─ NO → Is it a before/after groom photo?
│        ├─ YES → "Beautiful work!" Compliment only. Stay in your lane.
│        └─ NO → Skip it. You don't need to comment on everything.
│
You see a post asking about software. Should you mention GroomGrid?
│
├─ Is it Week 1?
│  ├─ YES → NO. Mention criteria, not products. (Template D)
│  └─ NO (Week 3+) → Only if directly asked. Always disclose.
│
Someone replies to your comment. What now?
│
├─ They're asking a follow-up question → Answer it helpfully. Be a resource.
├─ They're sharing their own experience → Validate it. Ask a follow-up.
├─ They're asking what you use → Week 1: "I've been testing a few things, still figuring it out." Week 3+: Disclose GroomGrid.
└─ They're being hostile → Do NOT engage. Move on.
```
