**Timing:** April 26 (Saturday), 11am MT (peak Reddit browsing time)
**Note:** This is a COMPLETELY different angle from the 3 post options in the existing Reddit plan. Those covered scheduling systems, no-show prevention, and pricing. This one covers the emotional/operational side of the first year.

---

**Title:** What I wish someone told me about the business side of grooming in my first year

I'm 5 years in now, and looking back at year one, I was SO focused on getting better at grooming that I completely neglected the business side. Figured I'd share some hard-learned lessons in case it helps someone starting out.

**Things that surprised me:**

**1. Your grooming skill is maybe 40% of your success.**
The rest is scheduling, client communication, payment collection, and record-keeping. Nobody tells you that in grooming school.

**2. "I'll just remember" is not a client management system.**
I thought I'd remember which dogs had allergies, which needed muzzles, which owners wanted updates mid-groom. I didn't. I almost used the wrong shampoo on a dog with sensitivities and that was my wake-up call.

**3. No-shows aren't a "cost of doing business" — they're a system problem.**
My first year I just... accepted that 15-20% of clients wouldn't show up. Turns out automated reminders cut that to under 5%. I lost thousands before I figured that out.

**4. Paper intake forms will betray you.**
The info you need is never on the form you have. And trying to read your own handwriting 6 months later? Good luck.

**5. You will spend more time on admin than you think.**
First year I estimated maybe 30 minutes a day on scheduling, invoicing, and follow-ups. It was more like 2 hours. That's 10 hours a week you're not getting paid for.

**6. Sunday nights will become planning anxiety.**
If your schedule lives in your head (or on sticky notes on your dash), you WILL spend Sunday nights dreading Monday. Every week.

**7. Chasing payments feels worse than you think.**
Asking someone to pay you for work you already did shouldn't feel awkward. But it does. Automating this was the single biggest quality-of-life improvement I made.

**What I do differently now:**
- Everything digital (no paper, no scattered notes)
- Automated reminders 24h and 2h before appointments
- Payment collected at booking or immediately after via automated link
- Pet profiles with allergies, behavior notes, and groom history all in one spot
- Buffer time between appointments (learned this the hard way)

**For anyone in year one:** what's been your biggest surprise about the business side? For the veterans: what would you add to this list?
