## Part 2: ICP Validation Interview Strategy

### Interview Target Profile

**Priority Tiers:**

**Tier 1 (Priority) — Mobile Groomers:**
- Solo mobile groomers
- 4-8 dogs/day
- High pain points (double bookings, no-shows, payments)
- Clear ROI case
- Faster sales cycle

**Tier 2 — Solo Salon Owners:**
- Decision maker + user
- 2-5 groomers total
- Staff scheduling pain points
- Medium sales cycle

**Tier 3 — Multi-Groomer Salons:**
- Longer sales cycle
- Bigger deal size
- Needs team features
- Lower priority for MVP

### Outreach Strategy

**Channel-Specific Outreach:**

**Facebook DM Outreach:**
```
Template: Post-Comment Follow-up

Hey [Name], loved your comment about [topic]!

I run GroomGrid — we actually automated that exact thing.
Would you be open to a 5-min demo? No pressure, just showing how other groomers solved it.

If it helps you, great. If not, no worries.

Best,
Sofia
```

**Reddit DM Outreach:**
```
Template: Value-First DM

Hey [Name], saw your post about [topic] and wanted to share something that helped me.

[Share specific, helpful advice]

Also, if you're ever looking to automate some of this stuff, I've been building a tool for groomers. Happy to give you free access for 30 days if you want to check it out.

No pressure, just thought it might help!

Best,
Sofia
```

**Email Outreach:**
```
Subject: 10-min research call: Grooming business challenges?

Hi [name],

I'm Sofia — former product manager researching software for grooming businesses.

Quick ask: Would you trade 10 minutes for a $25 Amazon gift card? I want to hear about your actual daily challenges — scheduling, no-shows, payments, client comms — not hypothetical stuff.

Questions like:
- What made you want to throw your computer out the window this week?
- What software have you tried and ditched? Why?
- What would actually make you switch from your current setup?

No sales pitch. Just research I'm doing to understand if we're solving real problems.

If you're interested: [Calendly link]

Either way, thanks for reading.
Sofia
```

### Interview Questions Framework

**Pre-Interview Scoping (2 mins):**
1. Are you mobile, salon-based, or both?
2. How many dogs do you groom per day? Per week?
3. Do you have employees or solo?
4. What software (if any) do you use currently?

**Pain Point Discovery (5 mins):**
1. Walk me through your yesterday — from waking up to done with work. Where did you get frustrated?
2. When did you last think "there HAS to be a better way to do this" — what was it about?
3. What's the task you procrastinate on because it's annoying?
4. How do you handle no-shows? What's that cost you per month?
5. Tell me about the last time a client annoyed you. What happened?
6. What takes longer than it should in your business?

**Solution Validation (3 mins):**
1. If software could fix ONE thing, what would you pay for?
2. What would make you switch from your current setup?
3. What made you choose your current software? What do you hate about it?
4. **Price check**: At $29/month for scheduling + reminders + payments — does that feel...
   - Too cheap (what's wrong with it?)
   - Just right
   - Too expensive (what would be fair?)
5. At what price would it be a "no-brainer"?

**Segmentation Questions:**
- How do you find new clients right now?
- What's your biggest fear about your business this year?
- What would make your life 10% easier?

## Part 5: Red Flags & Pivot Triggers

### Immediate Action Required (Red Flags)

**If ANY of these happen, pause and reassess within 24 hours:**

1. **Zero interview responses after 20 outreach messages**
   - Action: Revise outreach template, test different approach

2. **Facebook DM response rate < 10%**
   - Action: Revise DM templates, test different approaches

3. **Reddit post gets < 5 upvotes after 24 hours**
   - Action: Delete post, revise approach, try different topic

4. **Zero signups after 7 days of active engagement**
   - Action: Review all channels, test messaging, consider pivot

5. **Interviewees say pricing is way off**
   - Action: Conduct pricing survey, test different price points

### Warning Signs (Yellow Flags)

**Monitor these closely, adjust if trend continues:**

1. **Interviews scheduled but high no-show rate (>30%)**
   - Action: Improve reminder system, test different scheduling approach

2. **Single channel accounting for > 80% of engagement**
   - Action: Diversify, reduce dependency

3. **Pain points mentioned don't match our assumptions**
   - Action: Adjust product roadmap, validate with more interviews

4. **Community engagement but zero signups**
   - Action: Review conversion funnel, test different CTAs
